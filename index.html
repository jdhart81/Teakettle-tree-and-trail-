Our Nursery

Local Genetics, Locally Grown

Every tree in our nursery is sourced from Teakettle Mountain — regionally adapted stock that thrives in Montana soils and climate.

Grown Where They Belong

Our nursery sits on Teakettle Mountain outside Columbia Falls, where we propagate native seed stock that's been adapting to this specific climate and soil for generations.

When you plant a tree from our nursery, you're not just planting a tree — you're restoring a piece of the original Flathead Valley ecosystem.

Why local genetics matter:
Locally sourced trees have 2–3× better survival rates than nursery stock shipped from out of region, and support native pollinators and wildlife that co-evolved with these specific cultivars.
Conifer
Douglas Fir
Pseudotsuga menziesii

The backbone of the Rocky Mountain forest. Fast-growing, wildlife-supporting, and deeply rooted in local ecology.

Conifer
Western Red Cedar
Thuja plicata

Rich, aromatic, and long-lived. Ideal for privacy screens, riparian planting, and windbreaks. A Montana staple.

Deciduous Conifer
Tamarack / Western Larch
Larix occidentalis

The only deciduous conifer in the region — stunning gold in fall. Excellent for wet sites and wildlife corridors.

About Us

Born From the Mountain

Teakettle Tree and Trail grew out of a simple belief: the best trees for this valley are the ones that already grew here. We propagate from wild seed stock collected on Teakettle Mountain, outside Columbia Falls, where our family has worked the land for years.

We offer everything from nursery transplants to full landscape design, trail building, and natural timber construction — a complete land stewardship service rooted in local knowledge.

Whether you're restoring a degraded property, building a trail system, or planting an orchard, we bring the expertise, the stock, and the passion for this place to every project.

Colten Crowley Founder — Teakettle Tree and Trail, Columbia Falls, MT
